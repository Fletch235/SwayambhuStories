The Site

Recreating the
Swayambhu Temple

Swayambhu is one of the oldest religious sites in Nepal. It serves as a living monument visited by pilgrims and tourists for over 2,500 years and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Physical deterioration, limited accessibility, and the potential loss of contextual knowledge all threaten its long-term preservation. This project asks: what would it mean to digitally preserve the structure and the intangible aspects of the temple?

03 / 08
Methodology

Current
Approach

  • 1
    Capture

    20,000 photographs and 100 LiDAR scans, producing a 14GB architectural and local terrain mesh.

  • 2
    Reconstruct

    Imported into Unreal Engine 5.6. Also add Cesium for Unreal to generate 3d terrain for the surrounding area.

  • 3
    Annotate

    Create tour stations built using Unreal Engine Blueprints: info card, video and detail page per point of interest.

  • 4
    Deploy

    Packaged for Meta Quest 3, Windows, and Mac.

04 / 08
Obstacles

Challenges

  • 1
    Scale of the scan data

    The 20,000 image LiDAR mesh caused texture streaming pool overflow in UE5. We had to create smaller meshes to be compatible with Unreal Engine.

  • 2
    Grounding the reconstruction in its surroundings

    The scanned temple existed in isolation without the Kathmandu Valley terrain that gives Swayambhu its sense of place. Cesium for Unreal was integrated to help create a more immersive experience.

  • 3
    Immersion vs. information overload

    The annotation system had to educate the user without breaking immersion. Info cards never auto-dismiss, and all interactions are keyboard-driven so the cursor never interrupts the view.

  • 4
    Platform constraints

    Creating the project to be accessible as possible was a major consideration. This means creating an application that is lightweight. Meta Quest 3 also enforces strict memory and GPU constraints that affect user experience.

The Experience

Navigating
the application

The player approaches a point of interest. An info card pops up with a title and short description. Pressing E opens a detail page with video or long-form text. Pressing Q goes back. Walking away closes everything automatically.

The interaction is entirely keyboard-driven, preserving spatial presence throughout. No cursor, only a blur breaking the view.
